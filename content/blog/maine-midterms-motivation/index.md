## October has been an interesting month

I had every intention of doing more projects last month, but uh...life finds a way (or something). I mean, I _was_ productive, and I did actually take care of some tech things I wanted to, but nothing related to this blog. I've taken a deep dive into the IoT world, and started tinkering with some Hue bulbs and [Home Assistant](https://www.home-assistant.io/), an open source automation tool. While I've only created a few automations so far, I've been able to:

- trigger lights on/off based on certain conditions
- push everything through Apple's Homekit (even unsupported devices) for Siri voice control
- set up a Discord bot to send messages based on certin conditions

While all of it is pretty primitive, I plan on really using the Discord bot as a monitoring tool, probably tied in with Nagios or Grafana. My fiancee loves the idea of automatically turning things off when we leave, so it's a great "excuse" to delve deeper :)

---

I also took about a week and went to Acadia National Park in Maine. With everything going on in the world, it was a great getaway. No signal, next to no civilization - it was a great chance to unplug and unwind. Coming back, I had to prepare for the last midterms of my Associate's degree. No comp sci classes this semester, so I'm really struggling to motivate myself to complete it. Even though the classes are completely online, and I prefer the asynchronous aspect of it, I can't say the same for my professors who might be struggling a bit.

So even though I haven't done a lot of dev work, I've been busy I guess! I also copied all my personal projects off my work computer and onto my personal laptop. I'm excited to get back into things now and work on some new projects as well as polish my todo list app. Until next time!
